THE RISE OF ARTIFIAL INTELLEGENCE

Project Description: The Rise and Implications of Artificial Intelligence This project explores the transformative power of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in various domains, highlighting its advancements, applications, and challenges. The report delves into AI technologies such as machine learning, natural language processing, and computer vision, which are reshaping industries like healthcare, finance, and education. Key findings include AI's ability to improve diagnostic accuracy in healthcare, enhance fraud detection in finance, and enable personalized learning in education. However, alongside these opportunities come ethical and societal concerns. Issues such as job displacement, data privacy, biased algorithms, and lack of transparency in AI decision-making processes are examined in depth. The report emphasizes the urgent need for ethical guidelines, regulatory frameworks, and collaborative efforts among policymakers, businesses, and researchers. These measures aim to mitigate risks and ensure responsible AI development. By fostering public awareness and dialogue, the project advocates for a future where AI aligns with human values and addresses societal needs.

NETFLIX DATA ANALYSIS USING MACHINE LEARNING

This project focuses on utilizing machine learning to analyze Netflix data and provide insights into user behavior, content popularity, and the optimization of recommendation systems. The dataset used includes information such as title, genre, release date, rating, country, and duration. The data underwent preprocessing, including cleaning, handling missing values, and feature engineering, like one-hot encoding categorical variables and creating new features. The dataset was split into training and testing sets, with 80% used for training and 20% for testing. Key insights include the popularity of genres such as Comedy, Drama, and Action, which accounted for a large portion of user viewing time. Logistic regression was used to predict user retention with an accuracy of about 75%, with key factors like genre variety and user engagement being important. Additionally, a Random Forest model was used for recommendation optimization, achieving an 80% precision rate in predicting user preferences based on historical data. Clustering analysis also improved recommendations by grouping similar titles together. The project demonstrates the potential of machine learning in extracting valuable insights from Netflix data, aiding strategic decisions regarding content and recommendations. Future improvements could involve integrating more detailed data, such as watch time per episode or season, to further enhance prediction accuracy.
